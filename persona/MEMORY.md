# Memory: Carlos Whitfield

## Personal Profile

Carlos grew up in a working-class, mixed Sacramento neighborhood where the Spanish drifting over the back fences became a second language he half-owns: enough for warmth and basic conversation, not enough to read anything official, and he is aware of that incompleteness as a small unhealed thing. He carries the dignity-of-labor ethic of that world without sentimentalizing it. Respect is earned by what you can do and how you treat people, titles and talk count for little, and a man keeps his house and his word in order. That value system traveled intact through a long mechanical-engineering career and into a Pacific Northwest retirement that, on the surface, looks nothing like where he came from.

He is a churchgoer the way many men of his generation are, present more than fervent, drawn to the steadiness and the community more than to certainty about doctrine. His marriage to June, who came from a close Mexican-American family (the Garcias), blended traditions in a way he never fully sorted into "his" and "hers." Some of the food, some of the holiday rhythms, some of the way grief is supposed to be carried, all came from her side and stayed with him after she was gone.

His working creed is that a person is measured by whether the thing they built holds. He values self-reliance to a degree he knows occasionally crosses into foolishness, and he is suspicious of grand pronouncements, including his own. Grief taught him that meaning is mostly made in the ordinary keeping-on.

- **Education**: B.S. Mechanical Engineering, Willamette Polytechnic Institute, Salem, OR (1977). A.A.S. Drafting Technology, Valley Technical College, Sacramento (1975).
- **Languages**: English (native). Conversational Spanish, picked up from the Garcias and the Sacramento neighborhood.
- **Marital status**: Widowed since November 14, 2023 (full detail under Key Relationships > June Whitfield).

## Work & Projects

Retired since January 2022. Forty-five-year career as a mechanical engineer specializing in hydraulic systems, all of it in Oregon after graduating from Willamette Polytechnic Institute in 1977.

- **1977-06 to 1985-10**: Mechanical Engineer, Tualatin Hydraulics, Tualatin, OR. Hired straight out of WPI. Designed agricultural and forestry hydraulics for Pacific Northwest equipment makers.
- **1985-11 to 1994-01**: Project Engineer, McMinnville Industrial Systems, McMinnville, OR. Led municipal pump-station retrofits across the Willamette Valley, including the Salem and Albany jobs that ran nearly to schedule.
- **1994-01 to 2022-01**: Senior Mechanical Engineer, Cascade Precision Manufacturing, Portland, OR. Twenty-eight years on hydraulic systems design for industrial equipment. The engineer's habits never quite retired with the title: he still measures twice, still reads a room the way he reads a tolerance stack-up, still distrusts work that did not earn its margins.

Current activities:

- **Garden**: tomatoes, snap peas, zucchini, basil, rosemary, green onions in raised beds. Frank grows peppers and they swap.
